Financial Mathematics

Time Value of Money

Relationships used to compare costs and benefits occurring at different times.

Single Payment

Future and Present Value

$$F = P(1+i)^n$$
$$P = F(1+i)^{-n}$$

P is present value, F is future value, i is interest rate per period, and n is number of periods.

Uniform Series

Annualized Payments

$$F = A\left[\frac{(1+i)^n - 1}{i}\right]$$
$$P = A\left[\frac{(1+i)^n - 1}{i(1+i)^n}\right]$$
Compounding

Effective Interest Rate

$$i_{eff} = \left(1 + \frac{i}{m}\right)^m - 1$$

Use this when the quoted annual rate is compounded more frequently than once per year.

Decision Metrics

NPV, IRR, and AEC

Net Present Value and Rate of Return

NPV converts future cash flows into present dollars. A positive NPV generally indicates the project earns more than the required return.

$$NPV = \sum_{t=0}^n \frac{C_t}{(1+i)^t}$$
$$IRR:\quad NPV=0$$

Annual Equivalent Cost

AEC rewrites present costs as a uniform annual cost, making alternatives with different lifetimes easier to compare.

$$AEC = P\left[\frac{i(1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n-1}\right]$$

Nuclear Economics

LCOE and Fuel Cycle

Levelized Cost of Electricity

$$LCOE = \frac{\sum_t \frac{I_t + M_t + F_t + D_t}{(1+r)^t}}{\sum_t \frac{E_t}{(1+r)^t}}$$

Capital, O&M, fuel, waste/decommissioning, and electricity generation are discounted over the plant lifetime.

Fuel-Cycle Flow

Mine→Mill→Conversion→Enrichment→Fabrication→Reactor→Spent Fuel

Mass Balance

Feed, Product, and Tails

$$F=P+W$$
$$Fx_F=Px_P+Wx_W$$
$$\frac{F}{P}=\frac{x_P-x_W}{x_F-x_W}$$
Value Function

Separation Potential

$$V(x)=(1-2x)\ln\left(\frac{1-x}{x}\right)$$
SWU

Separative Work

$$S=PV(x_P)+WV(x_W)-FV(x_F)$$

SWU increases as product enrichment rises or tails assay decreases.
