Module 1

Crystal Structures

BCC

Body-centered cubic metals generally have lower packing density and can show lower ductility depending on temperature and alloy system.

FCC

Face-centered cubic metals are close-packed, have many slip systems, and often show high ductility.

HCP

Hexagonal close-packed materials have fewer easy slip systems and more anisotropic mechanical behavior.

Module 2

Diffusion

Fick's First Law

$$J=-D\frac{dC}{dx}$$

Use for steady-state diffusion.

Fick's Second Law

$$\frac{dC}{dt}=D\frac{d^2C}{dx^2}$$

Use for time-dependent diffusion.

Arrhenius Diffusion

$$D=D_0e^{-Q/(RT)}$$

Diffusion increases strongly with temperature.

Reactor Materials

Radiation Damage and Performance

Radiation Damage

Defects and DPA

Irradiation creates vacancies, interstitials, defect clusters, dislocation loops, and gas bubbles. Damage is often summarized using displacements per atom.

  • Point defects migrate and recombine.
  • Defect sinks include grain boundaries, surfaces, and dislocations.
  • Microstructural evolution changes strength, ductility, and swelling behavior.
Materials Performance

Void Swelling and Embrittlement

Radiation can cause void swelling, irradiation hardening, creep, phase instability, and embrittlement, all of which affect reactor material lifetime.
