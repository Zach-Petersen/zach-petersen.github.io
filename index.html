Research

High-Altitude Nuclear Explosions and Space Systems
EVIDENT Lab, University of Wisconsin–Madison
Modeling the effects of high-altitude nuclear explosions on space systems, including gamma-ray attenuation, satellite disruption, electromagnetic pulse generation, and charged particle trapping in the Van Allen belts. This work contributes to broader efforts to develop realistic nuclear war-effects simulation tools.
Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory
Defense Science and Technology Internship | Summers 2025 and 2026
Modeled far-field radiation transport from the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki to validate historical dose data. This work supported strategic deterrence and survivability analysis.
Oak Ridge National Laboratory
Research Internship
Conducted shielding analysis involving boron-containing systems and radiation transport applications.
