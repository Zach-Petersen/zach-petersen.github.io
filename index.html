Research

High Altitude Nuclear Exposions
Evident at University of Wisconsin–Madison Nuclear Engineering & Engineering Physics Department
Modeling the effects of high-altitude nuclear explosions including gamma attenuation, destruction of satellites, EMP creation, and electron trapping in the Van Allen Belts. This work is incorporated with a larger effort to create a nuclear war simulator that is a comprehensive view of the devastation that occurs from realistic war scenarios.
National Lab Internships
Lawerence Livermore National Laboratory
Student Researcher at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory – Defense Science and Technology Internship, Summer 2025 and Summer 2026. Modeled the far field radiation transport from war time bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki with the goal to validate historical dose data. This work supported strategic deterrence and survivability efforts at the laboratory.
