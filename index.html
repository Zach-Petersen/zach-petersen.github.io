About

I am a Nuclear Engineering student at the University of Wisconsin–Madison with research interests in nuclear security, nonproliferation, and nuclear war effects. My work spans computational modeling of radiation transport, shielding, and high-altitude nuclear environments using tools such as Python and MCNP.

I have conducted research at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory and Oak Ridge National Laboratory, contributing to dose validation, shielding analysis, and criticality calculations. I plan to pursue a Ph.D. in nuclear security to support global safety and risk reduction.

Research

High-Altitude Nuclear Explosions and Space Systems
EVIDENT Lab, University of Wisconsin–Madison
Modeling the effects of high-altitude nuclear explosions on space systems, including gamma-ray attenuation, satellite disruption, electromagnetic pulse generation, and charged particle trapping in the Van Allen belts. This work contributes to broader efforts to develop realistic nuclear war-effects simulation tools.
Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory
Defense Science and Technology Internship | Summers 2025 and 2026
Modeled far-field radiation transport from the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki to validate historical dose data. This work supported strategic deterrence and survivability analysis.
Oak Ridge National Laboratory
Summer Undergraduate Laboratory Internship | 2024
Used MCNP to perform shielding and criticality calculations for HALEU transportation canisters. Investigated source definitions and perturbations to the 30B-10 canister.

Publications

Petersen, Z., Gaylard, A., Lang, A., Celik, C., Metwally, W.
American Nuclear Society (ANS), 2024 • Conference Paper
“Perturbations to the 30B-10 Cylinder Criticality Control System.” [PDF] [Abstract]
This study investigates modifications to the 30B-10 cylinder criticality control system (CCS) to optimize HALEU fuel transport. Using MCNP simulations, the impact of control rod geometry and radius on keff and uranium mass capacity is analyzed. Results demonstrate that strategic removal and resizing of rods can increase U-235 capacity while maintaining safety constraints.
Petersen, Z., Gaylard, A., Lang, A., Celik, C., Metwally, W.
American Nuclear Society (ANS), 2024 • Conference Paper
“Improving Transport Efficiency of HALEU Fuel with a Modified Criticality Control System in the 30B-10.” [PDF] [Abstract]
This work evaluates how removing criticality control rods in the 30B-10 canister affects keff and increases HALEU transport capacity. A simplified analytical model is developed to approximate reactivity changes and uranium mass gain, enabling efficient design exploration prior to detailed Monte Carlo simulations.
